# Spell Mechanics

## Spell Basics

Every spell has the following properties:

- **Level** — 0 (cantrip) through 9. Higher levels = more powerful.
- **School** — Abjuration, Conjuration, Divination, Enchantment, Evocation, Illusion, Necromancy, Transmutation.
- **Casting Time** — 1 action, bonus action, reaction, or longer (1 minute, 10 minutes, etc.).
- **Range** — Self, touch, or a specific distance.
- **Components** — V (verbal), S (somatic), M (material); some M components have a gold cost and are consumed.
- **Duration** — Instantaneous, a fixed time, or Concentration.
- **Ritual** — Can be cast as a ritual (takes 10 extra minutes, doesn't expend a spell slot) if the caster has the ritual casting feature.

---

## Spell Slots

Spellcasters expend **spell slots** to cast spells of 1st level and higher. Cantrips (level 0) never use slots.

- A spell can be cast using a **higher-level slot** than its base level — many spells scale up (listed as "At Higher Levels").
- Spell slots are recovered on a **long rest** (for most classes). Warlocks recover slots on a **short rest**.

---

## Concentration

Some spells require **Concentration** to maintain.

- You can only concentrate on **one spell at a time**. Casting a new concentration spell ends the previous one.
- Taking damage requires a **Constitution saving throw** to maintain concentration:
  - DC = 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher.
- Automatically broken by: being incapacitated, dying, or casting another concentration spell.

---

## Spell Attacks

Some spells call for a **spell attack roll** instead of a saving throw.

**Roll:** `d20 + spellcasting ability modifier + proficiency bonus`

- Hits the target's AC.
- Natural 20 = critical hit (roll damage dice twice).

---

## Saving Throw Spells

Many spells allow a **saving throw** to resist or reduce their effects.

**Spell Save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + spellcasting ability modifier**

- Failure: Full effect (or as described).
- Success: Half damage, or no effect (as described by the spell).

---

## Cantrips

- No spell slot required.
- Many cantrips **scale with character level** (not class level):

| Character Level | Cantrip Damage Dice |
|----------------|---------------------|
| 1–4 | 1 die |
| 5–10 | 2 dice |
| 11–16 | 3 dice |
| 17–20 | 4 dice |

---

## Areas of Effect

| Shape | How It Works |
|-------|-------------|
| **Cone** | Originates from caster; width equals length at far end |
| **Cube** | Choose a point; the cube extends from there |
| **Cylinder** | Circular area, centered on a point, extends vertically |
| **Line** | Extends from caster or a point in a straight line |
| **Sphere** | Centered on a point; radius listed in spell |

A creature in an AoE only needs to be partially within the area to be affected.

---

## Combining Magical Effects

- **Different spells:** Their effects combine.
- **Same spell cast multiple times:** Only the most potent effect (your choice if equal) applies.
- **Spell + nonmagical effect:** They combine normally.

---

## Counterspell

Reaction; cast when a creature within 60 ft. casts a spell.

- **Spell level ≤ 3rd:** Automatically countered.
- **Spell level ≥ 4th:** Make a spellcasting ability check — DC 10 + spell's level. Success = countered.

Can itself be counterspelled.

---

## Ritual Casting

- Spells with the **ritual** tag can be cast without a spell slot, but take **10 extra minutes**.
- Only Wizards (and others with ritual books) can ritual-cast any spell they have in their spellbook. Clerics, Druids, and Bards must have the spell *prepared* to ritual cast it.

---

## Identifying Spells

- A spellcaster can identify a spell by using their **reaction** to make an Arcana check (DC 15 + spell's level) as it's being cast.
- The material component can also be a clue.

---

## Common Schools at a Glance

| School | Typical Effects |
|--------|----------------|
| Abjuration | Protection, wards, counterspells |
| Conjuration | Summoning, teleportation, creating objects |
| Divination | Information, scrying, foresight |
| Enchantment | Mind control, charm, compulsion |
| Evocation | Damage, elemental blasts, healing |
| Illusion | False images, invisibility, phantasms |
| Necromancy | Death, undead, life drain |
| Transmutation | Transformation, alteration, enhancement |
