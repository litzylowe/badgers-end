# Encounter Building Guide

## Challenge Rating (CR)

CR is a rough indicator of how difficult a monster is for a party of four level-appropriate adventurers.

- A monster's CR roughly equals the **party level** at which it's a suitable solo threat.
- CR 0 = trivial; CR 30 (Tarrasque) = most powerful creature in the game.

**Step 2:** Sum the XP of all monsters in the encounter.

## Monster Number Multiplier

Multiple monsters are more dangerous than their raw XP suggests.

| # of Monsters | Multiplier |
|---------------|-----------|
| 1 | ×1 |
| 2 | ×1.5 |
| 3–6 | ×2 |
| 7–10 | ×2.5 |
| 11–14 | ×3 |
| 15+ | ×4 |

*Also apply these multipliers if the party is 1–2 members (use the next higher multiplier) or 6+ members (use the next lower multiplier).*

## Encounter Structure Tips

- **One Boss + Minions:** Pair a powerful monster (CR = party level + 2–3) with several weak ones (CR ¼ to CR 1). Keeps the boss threatening while giving martial characters soft targets.
- **Equal Opposition:** 3–4 monsters of CR = party level − 1. Even, tactical fights.
- **Swarm:** Many low-CR monsters (CR ½ or less). Resource drain; can be overwhelming for low-level parties.
- **Solo Boss:** Use a monster with Legendary Actions/Resistance, or apply a CR ≈ party level + 3–4.

---

## Rest & Attrition

The game is balanced around approximately **6–8 encounters per long rest** and **2 short rests**. Fewer encounters make long-rest classes (Paladin, Cleric, Wizard) significantly stronger relative to short-rest classes (Warlock, Monk, Fighter).

---

## Environmental Hazards

Good encounters include terrain:
- **Elevation** — ranged attackers on high ground
- **Obstacles** — partial/full cover
- **Traps** — pressure plates, tripwires, magical wards
- **Moving hazards** — fire spreading, flooding, crumbling floor
- **Difficult terrain** — mud, rubble, shallow water

---

## Adjusting Encounters Mid-Session

- **Too easy:** Have reinforcements arrive; have the boss use a second-phase ability; increase monster aggression.
- **Too hard:** Have a monster flee; reduce HP on the fly; introduce an environmental escape route.
- **Party surprised:** If one side is surprised, they lose their first turn. This dramatically tilts the encounter — use sparingly.
