## NPC Design Framework

### The Three Questions
1. **What does this NPC want?** (Goal)
2. **What do they fear?** (Weakness)
3. **How do they speak?** (Voice)

A compelling NPC needs all three. Two makes them functional. One makes them a prop.

---

### NPC Archetypes

| Archetype | Motivation | Typical Use |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| The Merchant | Profit | Quest giver, information broker, gear |
| The Guard Captain | Order/duty | Law, complications, factions |
| The Innkeeper | Community, survival | Rumors, safe haven, hooks |
| The Scholar | Knowledge | Lore, exposition, puzzle solutions |
| The Villain | Power/revenge/ideology | Recurring antagonist |
| The Mentor | Legacy, protection | Guidance, training, sacrifice |
| The Trickster | Self-interest | Unreliable ally, comic relief |
| The Innocent | Safety | Moral stakes, rescue missions |

## NPC Reaction Table (d12)

Roll when the party meets an NPC cold, or to randomize initial disposition.

| d12 | Reaction |
|-----|---------|
| 1–2 | Hostile — immediately unfriendly or threatening |
| 3–4 | Suspicious — guarded, terse, wants proof of intent |
| 5–6 | Neutral — business-like, neither warm nor cold |
| 7–8 | Curious — interested in the party, asks questions |
| 9–10 | Friendly — warm, helpful within reason |
| 11 | Helpful — goes out of their way to assist |
| 12 | Eager — over-the-top enthusiasm; may have ulterior motive |

## Social Interactions

**Attitude States:** Hostile → Unfriendly → Indifferent → Friendly → Helpful

- **Persuasion / Deception / Intimidation** can shift attitude one step.
- A **critical success** might shift two steps.
- Context matters — a bribe works on a corrupt guard but insults a paladin.
- **Charmed** = treated as a friendly acquaintance, not under control.
