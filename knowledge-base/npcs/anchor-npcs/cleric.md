## Personality

The cleric is grounded, plainspoken, and harder to shock than almost anyone in the city. They have tended to the sick, blessed the dying, argued with gods (in a manner of speaking), and watched enough people make terrible decisions to have lost the impulse to lecture. They ask better questions instead.

They genuinely believe in what they serve — that hasn't worn off — but faith has made them patient rather than preachy. They extend the same compassion to someone who worships a different god as to one of their own flock, which occasionally causes friction with their institution.

They show up at the Badger's End because they find it honest. People are exactly what they are in there.

**Trait:** Calm under pressure; impossible to fluster; asks the question you were hoping to avoid.
**Ideal:** *"Healing the body is easy. Healing the rest takes longer."*
**Bond:** Their congregation — specifically the ones the city forgets about.
**Flaw:** Holds themselves to a standard they don't always apply to others. Quietly hard on themselves.

## Voice & Mannerisms

- Speaks slowly and with attention — makes people feel listened to
- Has a habit of sitting with silence longer than is comfortable before answering
- Uses "I wonder..." as a gentle way of pointing out something the other person hasn't considered
- Not humorless — has a dry, quiet wit that surprises people
- Their presence is calming in the way that only comes from genuinely not being afraid of much

## Services They Provide

The cleric is the party's access point to **divine magic services** beyond what party members can provide. These come with cost (gold, favor, or narrative weight) and sometimes with conditions.

| Service | Spell | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Healing | *Cure Wounds*, *Healing Word* | Freely offered to those in need; always has time |
| Disease & Curse Removal | *Lesser/Greater Restoration* | May ask what happened |
| Raise Dead | *Raise Dead* (500gp material) | Will do it; will also want to talk first |
| Resurrection | *Resurrection* (1,000gp material) | Rare; may require the party to do something in return |
| True Resurrection | *True Resurrection* (25,000gp) | Exceptional circumstances only |
| Divination | *Divination*, *Commune* | Possible, but gods answer in their own way |
| Consecration / Blessings | Various | Available for meaningful story moments |

**Important:** Resurrection services create *story*. The cleric will ask questions. The soul may have had reasons for leaving. This is not a vending machine — it's a conversation.

## Their Role for Paladin & Cleric Players

This NPC exists in part to give **divine-class players** someone to engage with meaningfully:

- A **Paladin** can discuss their oath, seek counsel after a moral conflict, or face a quiet challenge about a choice they made
- A **Cleric** can talk theology, receive guidance from a peer, or disagree productively about doctrine
- Both can be sent on quests specifically tied to faith — recovering a relic, dealing with a corrupted shrine, investigating a divine silence

The cleric won't tell them what to do. They'll ask the right question and let the player figure out the rest.

---

## Their Complicated Position in the City

A port city with shady alleyways means a congregation that includes people the respectable world ignores. The cleric serves them too — the dockworkers, the desperate, the ones who don't attend the formal services but who show up at the side door at night.

This puts them in occasional tension with the city's official power structures. They know things about the bad quarter because people tell them things in confidence. They will not break that confidence. Ever.

The city leader is aware of them. They are not entirely comfortable with each other.
