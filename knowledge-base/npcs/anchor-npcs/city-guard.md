## Personality

A guard who has worked this district long enough to know every face, every alley, and every excuse. Not corrupt — genuinely wants to keep the peace — but realistic about what the city guard can and can't do with the resources they have. The shadier parts of town are technically under their jurisdiction and practically out of their reach.

They like the Badger's End because it's one of the few places in the district that doesn't cause them problems. They trust Mr. H's judgment on who to worry about.

**Trait:** Straightforward and a little tired. Says what they mean.
**Ideal:** *"You can't fix everything. You fix what's in front of you."*
**Bond:** The district. These are their streets and they take that personally.
**Flaw:** Reluctant to ask for help — sees it as admitting defeat.

## Voice & Mannerisms

- Speaks plainly; doesn't dress things up
- Has a habit of scanning the room even when off duty
- Drinks slowly — never gets drunk in public
- Knows the name of every regular at the Badger's End
- Slightly uncomfortable asking the party for favors; does it anyway when necessary

---

## Their Role in the Campaign

The guard is the party's **bridge into city business**. They don't hire adventurers officially — the guard doesn't work that way. But off the record, over a drink, they might mention that something is happening in the bad quarter that they can't officially touch. Or that someone was reported missing and the paperwork is going nowhere. Or that the docks have been strange lately and they'd feel better if someone took a look.

**What they provide:**
- Local knowledge — who runs what, which areas are dangerous and why
- Low-stakes urban hooks that can escalate
- A friendly face in an institution — useful when the party gets into official trouble
- Legitimacy cover: *"I know these people. They're fine."*

---

## Their Limits

- Won't break the law or bend their oath, even for the party
- Can't officially sanction anything the party does in the shady quarter
- Has superiors with their own agendas — sometimes their hands are tied from above
- Gets quietly angry if the party causes collateral damage in their district
