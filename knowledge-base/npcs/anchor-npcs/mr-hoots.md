## Personality

Mr. Hoots does not have a personality so much as a *presence*. He observes. He appears. He watches people with the particular intensity of an owl that is either very bored or deeply interested — it is genuinely impossible to tell which.

He is not aggressive. He is not cuddly. He simply *is*, in whatever corner of the room he has decided to occupy this evening.

---

## His Thing

**Mr. Hoots likes to appear.** Not fly in — *appear*. Players will be mid-conversation and glance sideways to find him sitting on their shoulder, the table next to them, or the chair they were about to sit in. Nobody sees him arrive. Nobody sees him leave. He is simply somewhere he wasn't a moment ago.

**How to use this at the table:**
- Drop him into scene descriptions at unexpected moments: *"The chair across from you is occupied. Mr. Hoots stares at you."*
- Have him appear next to a specific player right before something goes wrong — the table will start reading him as an omen
- Occasionally have him *not* appear when players expect him, which is somehow worse
- Let players address him directly. He stares back. He does not respond. Or does he tilt his head?

## What Is He?

This is deliberately unanswered. Options for DMs to hint at, never confirm:

- Just an owl the previous owners kept, with no special nature
- A familiar whose master has long since disappeared
- A Fae creature who came back with Mr. H (they never acknowledge each other)
- A divine observer sent by a god with an interest in this place
- Something older than the building he lives in

**The rule:** No DM confirms his nature. Every DM gets to add one detail that deepens the mystery.

---

## Plot Hooks

- Mr. Hoots is gone. He's never gone. Where is he?
- He appears next to a new NPC before that NPC does something significant
- A wizard or druid in the party attempts to speak with him via magic — what does he say, if anything?
- Someone comes to the tavern specifically asking about "the owl" and won't say why
