## Personality

The merchant is sharp, sociable, and genuinely enjoys a good deal — even when they're on the losing end, if the story is good enough. They trade in goods but also in information, favors, and reputation. They have connections across the city and through the port, which means they hear about things before most people do.

They're not shady exactly, but they're flexible about where things come from as long as nobody makes it their problem.

**Trait:** Cheerful and transactional — approaches most situations like a negotiation.
**Ideal:** *"A fair exchange benefits everyone. An unfair one benefits me."* (Said with a grin.)
**Bond:** Their reputation. They've built it over years and guard it carefully.
**Flaw:** Can't resist an interesting deal, even when they should.

---

## Voice & Mannerisms

- Upbeat and quick — moves through conversation like they're closing a sale
- Always seems to know what you need before you ask
- Uses the phrase *"I might know someone"* a lot
- Dresses slightly better than the surroundings require
- Keeps mental notes on everyone they've ever done business with

---

## What They Sell

**Standard stock:** Adventuring gear, rope, torches, rations, basic weapons, light armor, tools, healing potions (at market price).

**Occasional specials:** Unusual items that "came off a ship" or "were part of an estate sale." DMs can use this to introduce one uncommon or interesting item per session if it serves the story.

**What they can source:** If the party needs something specific and has time (and coin), the merchant can usually find it — for a price and with a waiting period.

---

## Their Role in the Campaign

The merchant is the party's **economy anchor** — the person they sell loot to and buy supplies from. But they're also a **rumor node**. Goods flow through their hands from the docks, the market, and the road, and so does information.

**What they provide:**
- A consistent place to buy and sell without roleplaying a stranger every time
- Market rumors — *"Word is the trade road east has been disrupted. Caravans aren't getting through."*
- Port gossip — ships coming and going, unusual cargo, who arrived recently
- Occasional leads on where to find rare goods or specific people
