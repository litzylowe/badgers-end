# Combat Reference

## Turn Structure

Each combatant gets one **Action**, one **Bonus Action** (if available), one **Reaction** (if available), and **Movement** equal to their speed.

### Order of Play
1. **Surprise** — If a creature is surprised, it can't move or take actions on its first turn, and can't take reactions until that turn ends.
2. **Initiative** — Everyone rolls `d20 + Dexterity modifier`. Ties: higher Dex modifier goes first; further ties are broken simultaneously or by mutual agreement.
3. **Turns** — On your turn: move, take an action, bonus action, and free object interaction.
4. **Reactions** — Can be taken on your turn or another creature's turn (e.g., Opportunity Attacks, Shield spell).

---

## Actions in Combat

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Attack** | Make one melee or ranged attack (more with Extra Attack) |
| **Cast a Spell** | Cast a spell with a casting time of 1 action |
| **Dash** | Gain extra movement equal to your speed |
| **Disengage** | Movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks this turn |
| **Dodge** | Attacks against you have disadvantage; you have advantage on Dex saves |
| **Help** | Give an ally advantage on their next ability check or attack roll |
| **Hide** | Make a Dexterity (Stealth) check to become hidden |
| **Ready** | Prepare an action to trigger on a specified condition |
| **Search** | Devote attention to locating something |
| **Use an Object** | Interact with a second object, or with an object requiring special use |

## Attack Rolls

**Roll:** `d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient)`

- **Hit:** Roll equals or exceeds target's AC.
- **Critical Hit:** Natural 20 — roll all the attack's damage dice twice, then add modifiers normally.
- **Critical Miss:** Natural 1 — the attack automatically misses.

### Melee vs. Ranged
- **Melee attacks** use Strength (or Dex with Finesse weapons).
- **Ranged attacks** use Dexterity.
- Ranged attacks made while within 5 ft. of a hostile creature are made with **disadvantage**.

### Two-Weapon Fighting
When you take the Attack action with a light melee weapon, you can use a **bonus action** to attack with a different light melee weapon in your other hand. Add no ability modifier to the damage unless it's negative.

---

## Damage & Hit Points

- **Damage types:** Acid, Bludgeoning, Cold, Fire, Force, Lightning, Necrotic, Piercing, Poison, Psychic, Radiant, Slashing, Thunder.
- **Resistance:** Take half damage.
- **Immunity:** Take no damage.
- **Vulnerability:** Take double damage.

### Dropping to 0 HP
- Fall **unconscious** and begin making **Death Saving Throws** each turn.
- Roll a d20: 10+ = success, 9 or less = failure. 3 successes = stable. 3 failures = dead.
- A natural 20 = regain 1 HP. A natural 1 = counts as 2 failures.
- Taking damage at 0 HP = 1 failure (2 failures if from a critical hit).
- Instant kill: Damage that equals remaining HP + max HP kills outright.

---

## Opportunity Attacks

Triggered when a hostile creature **you can see** moves out of your reach.
- Use your **reaction** to make one melee attack against the creature.
- Dash, Disengage, or teleportation do **not** provoke opportunity attacks.

## Cover

| Type | AC & Dex Save Bonus |
|------|---------------------|
| Half cover (low wall, creature) | +2 |
| Three-quarters cover (portcullis, thick tree) | +5 |
| Full cover | Can't be targeted directly |

---

## Mounted Combat

- Mount must be willing, at least one size larger, and have appropriate anatomy.
- Mount acts on **your initiative** when you mount it.
- If you're knocked prone or the mount is, you can make DC 10 Dex save to land on your feet; fail = prone within 5 ft.

---

## Underwater Combat

- Melee weapons without the **thrown** property: disadvantage unless attacker has a swim speed.
- Ranged weapons: automatically miss beyond normal range; disadvantage within normal range.
- Fire damage: disadvantage on attack rolls.

---

## Common Combat Math Reminders

- **Flanking (optional rule):** Granting advantage when two allies are on opposite sides of a creature.
- **Grapple:** Attack action, Athletics vs. Athletics or Acrobatics. Target's speed becomes 0. Size must be no more than one larger than you.
- **Shove:** Attack action, Athletics vs. Athletics or Acrobatics. Target is knocked prone OR pushed 5 ft.
