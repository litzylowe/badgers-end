# Skills & Ability Checks

## Ability Checks

**Roll:** `d20 + ability modifier (+ proficiency bonus if proficient)`

Beat the **Difficulty Class (DC)** to succeed.

### Difficulty Class Guide

| Difficulty | DC |
|------------|----|
| Very Easy | 5 |
| Easy | 10 |
| Medium | 15 |
| Hard | 20 |
| Very Hard | 25 |
| Nearly Impossible | 30 |

| Ability | Governs |
|---------|---------|
| **Strength (STR)** | Physical power, melee, carrying |

## Skills

Each skill is tied to an ability score. Proficiency adds your **Proficiency Bonus** to rolls.

### Strength
- **Athletics** — Climbing, jumping, swimming against currents, grappling

### Dexterity
- **Acrobatics** — Balance, tumbling, escaping grapples
- **Sleight of Hand** — Pickpocketing, concealing objects
- **Stealth** — Moving silently and unseen

### Intelligence
- **Arcana** — Magic, spells, planes, magical creatures
- **History** — Events, legends, wars, ancient civilizations
- **Investigation** — Deducing clues, finding hidden compartments, research
- **Nature** — Terrain, plants, animals, weather, natural cycles
- **Religion** — Deities, rites, holy symbols, undead, cults

### Wisdom
- **Animal Handling** — Calming animals, controlling mounts, reading animal intent
- **Insight** — Detecting lies, reading emotions and motives
- **Medicine** — Diagnosing illness, stabilizing creatures
- **Perception** — Noticing things with sight, hearing, or other senses
- **Survival** — Tracking, foraging, navigating, predicting weather

### Charisma
- **Deception** — Lying convincingly, disguises, misleading
- **Intimidation** — Threats, hostile pressure, coercion
- **Performance** — Entertainment, music, acting, storytelling
- **Persuasion** — Diplomacy, honest requests, social grace

## Advantage & Disadvantage

- **Advantage:** Roll 2d20, take the **higher** result.
- **Disadvantage:** Roll 2d20, take the **lower** result.
- Multiple sources of advantage/disadvantage don't stack — you either have it or you don't.
- Advantage and disadvantage **cancel each other out** regardless of how many sources of each exist.

---

## Passive Checks

A passive check is `10 + all modifiers that normally apply`. Used when the DM wants to determine what a character notices without calling for a roll (e.g., Passive Perception).

- If a character has **advantage**, add 5.
- If they have **disadvantage**, subtract 5.

---

## Saving Throws

Saving throws use ability modifiers + proficiency bonus (if proficient in that save).

| Save Type | Common Triggers |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Strength** | Being physically moved against your will |
| **Dexterity** | Area-of-effect spells (fireball, etc.) |
| **Constitution** | Maintaining concentration, resisting poison/disease |
| **Intelligence** | Mind-affecting magic (rare) |
| **Wisdom** | Charm, fear, illusion magic |
| **Charisma** | Banishment, possession, some planar magic |

---

## Contests

When two creatures compete directly, both roll the relevant check. The higher result wins. Ties mean no change (status quo holds).

*Examples: Grapple (Athletics vs. Athletics/Acrobatics), Sneak vs. Perception (Stealth vs. Perception), Lie vs. Read (Deception vs. Insight)*

---

## Group Checks

When the whole party attempts something together, everyone rolls. If at least **half succeed**, the group succeeds. Useful for group Stealth, foraging, etc.

---

## Tool Proficiency

Proficiency with a tool allows you to add your proficiency bonus to ability checks using that tool. The DM sets which ability applies based on the task.

*Common tools: thieves' tools, herbalism kit, healer's kit, forgery kit, disguise kit, musical instruments, artisan's tools*
