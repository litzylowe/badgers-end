# The Temple Network — "The Light"

> *They serve everyone. That's what makes them dangerous.*

---

## Overview

| | |
|-|-|
| **Nickname** | The Light |
| **Anchor NPCs** | The Cleric |
| **Power Base** | Divine magic, public goodwill, information from all walks of life |
| **Public Goal** | Healing, spiritual guidance, care for the city's poor |
| **Hidden Reality** | Knows more about this city than any other faction — because everyone talks to a healer |

---

## What They Want

To serve their deity and the city's people — genuinely. The Temple is one of the few factions whose public goal and private goal actually align. What complicates things is that serving everyone means knowing everyone's secrets, which makes the Temple quietly one of the most powerful information networks in the city without ever intending to be.

They want the city to be better than it is. They are patient about this in a way that unnerves people.

---

## What They Control

- Healing services — the only reliable source of resurrection in the city
- The loyalty of the poor quarter and dockworkers
- Information that flows through confession, bedside manner, and community trust
- A network of shrines and smaller temples throughout the city
- Moral authority — difficult to attack publicly without losing popular support

---

## What They Can't Control

- Funding — they depend on donations and the city's occasional goodwill
- Political power — they refuse to play faction games overtly, which limits their leverage
- Their own people — not every acolyte shares the cleric's wisdom or restraint

## Player Allegiance — The Light

**How to earn renown:** Help the sick and poor, protect temple members, recover stolen relics, deal with corrupted shrines, support the cleric's community work.

**Benefits by renown:**

| Renown | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| 1–2 | Healing potions at cost; the cleric will see you quickly |
| 3–5 | Free lesser restoration services; access to temple library |
| 6–8 | Resurrection services at reduced cost; safe haven in the temple |
| 9–11 | The Temple's information network available on important questions; divine contacts |
| 12+ | Full resurrection at cost only; temple speaks publicly on your behalf |

**Allegiance cost:** The Temple expects the party to behave with some moral consistency. They don't demand perfection — but causing civilian harm, desecrating holy sites, or working actively against the poor quarter damages standing deeply.

**Special — Divine Class Bonus:** Paladin and Cleric PCs gain +1 renown with The Light automatically if they share the cleric's faith or a compatible one.

---

## Adventure Hooks

- Something is wrong at a shrine in the poor quarter — people are stopping going and won't say why
- A temple acolyte has gone missing in the shady quarter
- The cleric has been asked to perform a ritual by the city leader that conflicts with their faith
- Someone powerful is dying and has asked for the cleric — and someone else very much doesn't want that conversation to happen
- A relic was stolen from the temple; it's dangerous in the wrong hands

---

## DM Notes

The Temple's power comes from what they *know*, not what they *do* with it. A good scene with the cleric often involves them gently surfacing information the party needed without quite stating it — asking the right question, noting that a certain person seemed frightened last week, mentioning that donations from the merchant district dropped off recently.

They are a compass, not a sword.
