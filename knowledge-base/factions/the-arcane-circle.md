# The Arcane Circle — "The Quill"

> *Knowledge belongs to those who can understand it. Everyone else is just a liability.*

---

## Overview

| | |
|-|-|
| **Nickname** | The Quill |
| **Anchor NPCs** | The Wizard |
| **Power Base** | Magical knowledge, arcane services, rare materials, information |
| **Public Goal** | Research, scholarship, the responsible practice of magic |
| **Hidden Reality** | Politically neutral in theory; in practice, whoever controls information controls everything |

---

## What They Want

To be left alone to pursue knowledge — and to be consulted, compensated, and credited when that knowledge proves useful to others. The Arcane Circle is not interested in governing the city. They are deeply interested in not being governed themselves.

Beneath the academic detachment, individual members have their own ambitions. The Circle as an institution tries to manage these.

---

## What They Control

- Magical services in the city — identification, enchantment, ritual magic
- A library of accumulated research not available elsewhere
- Expertise on arcane threats, magical creatures, and extraplanar entities
- The training and certification of spellcasters operating in the city
- Rare components and magical materials through their trade networks

---

## What They Can't Control

- Their own members' curiosity — research has a way of going sideways
- The city's desire to regulate magic — a recurring political fight
- The Fae connection that some members have been quietly investigating

---

## Internal Tensions

The Circle has two broad camps:
- **The Institutionalists** — believe the Circle's power comes from its reputation for responsibility; want to cooperate with the city enough to maintain autonomy
- **The Independents** — believe knowledge has no political obligations; will research whatever interests them regardless of who's uncomfortable

The wizard anchor NPC leans Independent — too distracted by curiosity to be politically strategic, which the Institutionalists find exhausting.

## Player Allegiance — The Quill

**How to earn renown:** Recover lost magical knowledge, assist with research, deal with arcane threats, bring back rare materials or samples, protect Circle members or assets.

**Benefits by renown:**

| Renown | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| 1–2 | Spell identification at cost; access to basic Circle library |
| 3–5 | Research assistance on one topic; components at cost |
| 6–8 | Use of the Circle's full library; access to a ritual specialist |
| 9–11 | A Circle member will accompany the party on one significant mission |
| 12+ | Access to restricted research; the Circle advocates for the party officially |

**Allegiance cost:** Destroying magical knowledge, harming Circle members, or selling Circle research to outside parties damages standing severely.

**Special — Arcane Class Bonus:** Wizard, Sorcerer, and Warlock PCs gain access to one additional spell for their spellbook/list when reaching renown 5+ (Circle-approved research benefit).

---

## Adventure Hooks

- The wizard's current research has attracted something dangerous
- A magical experiment went wrong somewhere in the city — the Circle wants it handled quietly before the city finds out
- A rogue mage is operating in the city without Circle oversight, and what they're doing is alarming
- Rare materials were stolen from the Circle's stores — they suspect an inside job
- An ancient text surfaced in the market — the Circle will pay significantly for it, and so will someone else

---

## DM Notes

The Arcane Circle is the party's access point to magical lore and expertise. Use them to explain things the party can't figure out themselves — but always make the knowledge come with a cost, a complication, or a new question. Information from the Circle should open doors, not close them.

The wizard anchor NPC can represent the Circle without being its spokesperson — they're too chaotic for that. But through them, the party gets a feel for what the Circle values and how it thinks.
