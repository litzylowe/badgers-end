# The Merchant Guild — "The Coin"

> *Money is just power with better manners.*

---

## Overview

| | |
|-|-|
| **Nickname** | The Coin |
| **Anchor NPCs** | The Merchant |
| **Power Base** | Trade, the port, guild membership, money |
| **Public Goal** | Fair trade, economic growth, protection of merchants' interests |
| **Hidden Reality** | Funds the city, controls the port's legitimate traffic, and has made arrangements with the Underworld it doesn't discuss |

---

## What They Want

To control the flow of goods through this city — and by extension, the city itself. The Merchant Guild doesn't want to *run* the government; that's messy and expensive. They want to *fund* it, which is quieter and more effective. The city does what the Guild needs because the Guild holds the purse strings.

The docks are their crown jewel and their constant headache — profitable, essential, and shared uncomfortably with the Underworld.

---

## What They Control

- The majority of the city's legitimate trade
- The port's official operations — docking fees, cargo inspection, shipping lanes
- Guild membership, which affects who can legally operate a business in the city
- Political donations and the leverage that comes with them
- Trade routes in and out of the city by land and sea

---

## What They Can't Control

- The Underworld's parallel economy — they've tried
- The Temple's independence — the Guild can donate but not direct
- The Arcane Circle's pricing on magical goods — an ongoing frustration
- What actually comes off ships at night

---

## Internal Tensions

The Guild is not monolithic. Three loose factions compete within it:
- **The Shipping Houses** — old money, port-focused, some with Underworld ties they'd deny
- **The Market Traders** — mid-tier merchants, want fairer guild rules, suspicious of the Shipping Houses
- **The New Interests** — recent arrivals with outside connections; the established guild eyes them carefully

The merchant anchor NPC sits somewhere in the middle — connected enough to be useful, independent enough to not be captured by any one group.

## Player Allegiance — The Coin

**How to earn renown:** Protect Guild shipments, resolve trade disputes favorably, retrieve stolen goods, expand Guild interests, deal with Underworld interference at the docks.

**Benefits by renown:**

| Renown | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| 1–2 | 10% discount with the merchant anchor NPC; intro to other Guild traders |
| 3–5 | Access to Guild information network (trade rumors, ship manifests); 15% discount |
| 6–8 | Guild letter of credit (spend now, settle later); access to rare goods |
| 9–11 | Guild backing in legal disputes; a ship and crew available for one job |
| 12+ | Full partnership — a share in a trading venture; significant political leverage |

**Allegiance cost:** The Guild expects results and discretion. Disrupting legitimate trade, damaging Guild property, or helping the Underworld undercut Guild operations hurts standing significantly.

---

## Adventure Hooks

- A Guild shipment has gone missing — the cargo manifest is vague about what exactly was in it
- A new merchant in the city is undercutting Guild members impossibly — the Guild wants to know how
- Something came off a ship that shouldn't have, and the Shipping House that owns the vessel is being very quiet about it
- A Market Trader asks the party to help expose corruption in the Shipping Houses — off the record
- The Guild needs someone to open a new trade route through territory that's currently too dangerous

---

## DM Notes

The Merchant Guild is at its most interesting when its internal divisions are visible. The party might help one faction within the Guild in one session and find themselves at odds with another in the next. "The Guild" is never one unified voice — it's a negotiation that's always ongoing.
