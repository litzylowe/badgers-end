# The City — "The Shield"

> *Official authority. Public face. Carefully maintained fiction.*

---

## Overview

| | |
|-|-|
| **Nickname** | The Shield |
| **Anchor NPCs** | City Leader, City Guard |
| **Power Base** | Law enforcement, military, taxation, permits and licenses |
| **Public Goal** | Order, prosperity, and the city's good reputation |
| **Hidden Reality** | Squeezed between the Merchant Guild's money and the Underworld's quiet arrangements |

---

## What They Want

A stable, prosperous city that runs smoothly enough that nobody looks too closely at how. The city leader is not naive — they know the Underworld exists, they know the Merchant Guild has more power than it should, they know the Hidden Council is real. They work within these constraints because the alternative is chaos.

They want the party to be useful without being inconvenient.

---

## What They Control

- The city guard — patrols, arrests, official investigations
- Taxation and the city treasury
- Permits for businesses, ships, and gatherings
- The official response to external threats
- Public perception — they control the narrative when things go wrong

---

## What They Can't Control

- The shady quarter, practically speaking
- What happens on the docks after dark
- The Merchant Guild's political influence
- The Temple's loyalty — the cleric answers to a higher authority
- The Hidden Council, though the city leader may not know the extent of this

---

## Relationship to Other Factions

| Faction | Relationship |
|---------|-------------|
| The Temple | Uneasy cooperation — they need each other but trust is thin |
| The Merchant Guild | Dependent — the Guild funds the city; the city grants the Guild privileges |
| The Arcane Circle | Suspicious tolerance — the city wants oversight; the Circle refuses it |
| The Underworld | Managed coexistence — officially at war, practically in negotiation |
| The Hidden Council | *[Unknown to most — the city leader may be a puppet or a member]* |

---

## Player Allegiance — The Shield

**How to earn renown:** Help the guard, resolve public threats quietly, protect the city's reputation, work within official channels.

**Benefits by renown:**

| Renown | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| 1–2 | The guard contact vouches for you; minor legal trouble overlooked |
| 3–5 | Access to city records and official information; guard backup on request |
| 6–8 | Letters of authority; ability to deputize; city resources in emergencies |
| 9–11 | Audience with the city leader; significant legal protection |
| 12+ | Trusted with city secrets; influence over guard priorities |

**Allegiance cost:** The city expects discretion. High-profile chaos, civilian harm, or working openly with the Underworld damages standing significantly.

---

## Adventure Hooks

- The city leader quietly needs something handled that can't go through official channels
- A guard is taking bribes from the Underworld — someone needs to find out before it goes higher
- A trade dispute is about to become a political crisis and the city needs capable people
- Official records have been altered — someone inside has been bought

---

## DM Notes

The City is most interesting when it's *not* corrupt in a cartoonish way. They're doing their best within a flawed system. The city leader has made compromises they believe were necessary. The guard contact is genuinely trying. That makes them more compelling than a simple authority to rebel against — and more painful to disappoint.
